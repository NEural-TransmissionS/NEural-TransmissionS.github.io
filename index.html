Space perception and autonomy, explainable AI and neural representations, and applied machine learning

The NEural TransmissionS (NETS) Lab builds machine learning systems for scientific and engineering problems where off-the-shelf methods break down. Our work focuses on computer vision, representation learning, and explainable AI, with an emphasis on understanding what models are actually learning—not just how well they perform.

A central application area is space-based perception: enabling inspection, tracking, and 3D reconstruction of spacecraft in challenging orbital environments. These are safety-critical settings, where models must generalize reliably, be understood, and operate under strict computational constraints at deployment.

Work in the lab spans theory, modeling, and deployment, with students working alongside collaborators from government and defense labs, commercial partners, and academic groups across disciplines. Projects involve contributors from high school through PhD working on shared research problems.

Research Areas

Space Perception and Autonomy

Computer vision and learning systems for inspection, tracking, navigation, and 3D reconstruction of spacecraft in challenging orbital environments.

Explainable AI and Representations

We study what deep models encode internally and develop methods to shape, interpret, and extract useful structure from learned representations.

Digital Twins and Sensing

Orbital digital twins, controllable synthetic sensor feeds, auto-annotation pipelines, and mixed real/sim experimental systems for rapid vision development.

Scientific and Engineering ML

Collaborative machine learning projects in science and engineering, including applications in quantum biology, aviation meteorology, and other data-rich domains.
