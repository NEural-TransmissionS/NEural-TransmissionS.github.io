What this demo shows

This is a one-pixel color challenge. Adjust the blobs and control how light flows through them until the final pixel matches the target color.

That same basic idea is part of 3D Gaussian Splatting: many soft, colored blobs work together to generate 2D images of 3D objects from a camera view. It is also a tiny example of raytracing: following a path of light through a 3D world to decide what a pixel should look like.

Ideas like this show up across graphics, robotics, and space systems, including video games, self-driving cars, spaceflight navigation, satellite inspection, drones, and search-and-rescue.
