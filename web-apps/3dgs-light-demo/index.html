How the light gets its color

A 3D Gaussian is like a soft glowing blob in space. A camera ray flies through the blobs on its way to one pixel. When the ray leaves a blob, it picks up color from the spot where it comes out.

Your job is to shape, turn, and recolor the blobs until the ray comes out matching the target. The ray disappears inside each blob, then reappears with its new color so you can see the pixel being built step by step.
